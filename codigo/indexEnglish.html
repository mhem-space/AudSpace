What is surround sound?

After watching the video and having experienced a bit of the difference between stereo audio and surround sound, it's my turn to explain a bit about how it works. For this, I will use 8D music as an example, a genre that has been gaining popularity over the last few years.

8D audio is a sound processing technique that has become popular in recent seasons. This technique uses a set of audio processing techniques, such as reverberation, echo and diffusion, to create an immersive and enriching sound experience for the listener.



How is the surround sound effect created in 8D audio?

The 8D effect is achieved by creating a sense of movement and depth in the sound, giving the impression that the sound is surrounding the listener. This is achieved through the use of sound mixing and editing techniques, such as panning, to give the sensation of movement to the music: placing the different audio tracks in different positions in virtual space allows the music to appear to move in a circle around the listener. In addition to the one mentioned above, another effect that is often used is the layering of sound layers. These effects are added in post-production, once the song has been recorded in the traditional way.

In addition to 8D, we can find 9D, 4D, 6D and many other styles. The "D" in the name stands for dimensions. The greater the number of dimensions, the greater the degree of audio alteration.



8D audio application to the music industry

8D audio has been used in different genres of music, from electronic music to pop and rock. Many artists have experimented with the technique to create 8D versions of their songs and offer a more enriching listening experience to their fans. In addition, 8D audio has also been used in album and song production, allowing producers to create a more immersive and dynamic sound.

Overall, the use of 8D audio in music has contributed to an improvement in the quality and immersion of the listening experience, offering a more enriching way to enjoy music.
